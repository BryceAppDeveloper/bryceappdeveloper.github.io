What is an FLHA?

A Field Level Hazard Assessment (FLHA) — also called a Job Hazard Analysis (JHA) — is a short form the crew completes at the start of each working day, before tools come out. You list the day's tasks, the hazards each one creates, and the controls that eliminate or reduce them. Everyone working on site signs it.

Why every day?

Job sites change daily — new trades, new equipment, different weather, different work. A two-minute assessment each morning catches the hazard that wasn't there yesterday. Most jurisdictions expect a documented daily hazard assessment; this form uses a universal format that covers what FLHA-style assessments commonly require. Requirements vary by region, so check your local OHS/OSHA rules for anything extra.

FLHA quick answers

Who fills out an FLHA? Usually the supervisor or lead hand starts it, but it's a crew exercise — everyone reviews the hazards and signs.

When is an FLHA done? Before work starts, every working day — and again if conditions change significantly (new task, new equipment, weather turn).

How long should an FLHA take? A focused 2–5 minutes. If it takes longer, the form is fighting you — which is exactly why we built the app below.
