Welcome & how MorForm thinks

Three apps, one paper trail, and every list starts empty until your work fills it.

The suite

Three apps, one paper trail

MorForm is three apps that pass work between them: Office on the estimating desk, Manager on the PM's phone, and Field on the supervisor's. This course walks one job through all three.

MorForm Office

Windows desktop

The estimating desk. Estimates, client quotes, jobs, dispatch, and the company dashboard.

MorForm Manager

iOS & Android

The PM's site tool. Crew, job specs, dispatching work, incoming field reports, and a day-timer.

MorForm Field

iOS & Android

The supervisor's logbook. Hours, expenses with receipts, safety forms, and reports back to the PM.

The one big idea

Every list starts empty

MorForm ships with no demo data. All of the dropdown menus start off empty and populate as you use MorForm: add a client once and every client picker knows them, name a division once and it's a suggestion next time. The first setup screen already works this way: pick Canada and the province list appears; pick Alberta and for the whole suite are set. Every safety form from here on quotes the Alberta OHS Act.

No accounts, no servers

Your work moves as files

There is no MorForm account and no MorForm server. Work travels as plain files you can see, keep, and back up:

.morjob: a dispatched job.morrep: a field report.mordata: a full handoff

Send them by email, or connect a shared Drive / Dropbox / OneDrive folder that you own and the apps pass files automatically. All of it lives in Office's Data menu:

  • : Import Job (.morjob) opens a job package another MorForm app sent you. Import Report (.morrep) pulls a supervisor's field report into its job. Import / Send Data to Manager (.mordata) moves estimates, jobs and contacts between Office and the PM's phone in one file.
  • : Connect / Change points the suite at your shared folder, Check Cloud for New Files pulls anything the crew dropped in, Disconnect turns it off, and How to Set Up Company Cloud is the built-in guide.
  • : Back Up All Data writes everything to a single .morbak file; Restore Backup reads one back. One file, kept wherever you keep files.

That's the theory. Next: install Office and set it up.

Estimating

Start blank or from a template, name divisions in plain language, and let the sheet do the math.

Two ways to start

Blank sheet or template library

New Estimate asks one question: start from Blank or from a template. ships with General Contractor (Commercial / Residential) and trade templates for Plumbing, Electrical, Structural, Interior Systems and HVAC. Templates copy the division and line-item structure, and sheets you save as templates join the same list.

The authoring canvas

Divisions and line items, typed in place

A blank estimate is a live sheet. runs across the top: Draft → Approved → Quoted → Won. Under it are client and markup fields, , and with ghost prompts. Name divisions in your own words, like "Framing" or "Sitework & Foundation".

The math

Labour lines calculate, totals roll up

Labour rows multiply Workers × Hrs × Days × Rate. : 1 worker × 8 hrs × 9 days at $65 works out to $4,680. Material and sub rows take dollar amounts. Default Markup is set once in the header; every row inherits it (the ↑ in its Markup% box) and any row can be typed over. recalculates on every keystroke. The finished Riverbend sheet: four divisions, nine line items, $103,185.60. Note .

Every line has a Contractor/Vendor field with a "Choose from contacts" picker, and it lists only what you've saved. Summit Framing went in back in Part 2, so . Typed once, picked from now on.

Make it yours

Reshape the sheet, save your own template

Edit Layout lists every column with a plain note on what it does. Rename them, reorder them, hide the ones you don't use, or : Equipment $, Contingency %, a Room note. When the sheet fits how you work, and it joins the start-from list. Start from one later and : structure copies, prices never do.

Estimate to won job

Approve it, quote it, win it. The job creates itself, and your costs never leave the building.

Step 1

Approve it internally

Approve (internal) is your own sign-off that the numbers are ready. Nothing goes to the client. , and : Proposal & Quote, Mark Quoted, or Back to Draft if you change your mind. Mark Quoted skips the PDF, for when the client already has your price some other way.

Step 2

Build a clean quote, keep costs private

Proposal & Quote builds the client PDF. You choose how much price detail the client sees: one grand total, section subtotals (the usual pick), or every line item. The quote number is assigned automatically. Export asks where to save the PDF; sending it is up to you, from your own email. The dialog states the rule: internal costs (labour math, markup, vendors) are never included.

Quote numbers are yours to run: Settings → PDF & Quotes. Auto assigns Q-YYYY-NNN, with an optional restart at 001 each new year. Manual asks you to type a number at each export. Override next number re-points the counter once; numbering continues from there.

Step 3

Won → the job creates itself

Generating the quote moves the estimate to Quoted. When the client says yes, hit Won → Create Job: the job dialog opens pre-filled from the estimate: name, address, scope notes. You just add start and end dates. .

Job & costs

The supervisor's phone: import the dispatched job, then log the hours and receipts that become the report.

Onboarding

Set up once, work all season

Field onboarding is the worker's side of setup, done once. It asks for the company and the worker's name (the name that signs every form), a pay schedule so the timesheet groups pay periods correctly, work hours and a timesheet increment (the steps the Hours dropdown counts in), and a jurisdiction, which sets the overtime rules and the OHS act quoted on every safety form.

The dispatch lands

Import the job. Nothing re-typed

The .morjob the PM sent opens straight into the app: PM contact, site note, the building permit, the client contact. The whole package lands intact. appear on the card on their own; they're compliance prompts the app raises, not decoration.

Time

Hours in the increment you chose

Add Entry → pick the job (the dropdown holds only the jobs on this phone) → set the hours in your increment → flag overtime or a stat holiday → save. A green dot marks the day; the week adds itself up.

Expenses

Receipts photographed at the counter

An expense takes a job, a payment method (company card, vendor account, or personal to reimburse later), a vendor, and a receipt photo from camera or library. Blurry receipt? swaps it without redoing the form. Watch : $147.75 at Site Supply Co., receipt attached. Half-finished forms get a discard guard, so a phone call doesn't cost you the entry.

MorForm FieldPart 9 of 10~6 min

Safety done right

FLHA, site inspection, emergency response plan: guided, OHS-compliant, and signed on the spot.

The toolkit

One tab, the whole safety routine

The Safety tab holds it all: FLHA, Site Hazard Inspection, Incident Report, Emergency Response Plan, plus blank printable forms and paper-form upload. Each one is tagged with the OHS rule it satisfies. If something does go wrong, the Incident Report is right there in the same list: injury, near miss, or dangerous occurrence. And does the nagging: "FLHA Not Yet Done Today."

FLHA

Hazard assessment, step by step

The FLHA is five guided stages. Job info pulls from your job list, hazards are tap-to-select chips, task steps build a reusable library, PPE is a checklist, and the crew signs on the glass. The attestation quotes your jurisdiction: "…per Alberta OHS Act… all workers understand their right to refuse unsafe work." .

FLHA: five stages

1 Job Info→2 Site Hazards→3 Task Steps→4 PPE→Review & Sign

The core lesson

Every hazard forces a real control

Select a hazard and a Control Methods card opens for it, ranked by the OHS Hierarchy of Controls, 1 Eliminate down to 5 PPE. Slippery Surfaces takes plus . Ticking a box isn't enough.

The form says it in so many words: "PPE alone is not sufficient." Controls first, equipment last.

Inspection

The site inspection scores itself

The 12-page Site Hazard Inspection rates every item Pass / Fail / N/A / Action Required. Any non-Pass opens a mandatory "describe the issue" field, a live tally rides in the header, and Action-Required items roll into a punch list with your note attached. .

ERP

The plan you post before work begins

The Emergency Response Plan (OHS Code Part 7) pre-fills 911, Alberta OHS and Poison Control, then asks for what only you know: nearest hospital and route, muster point, first-aid locations, emergency contacts. Review it with the crew, sign, post it.

Settings across the suite

Every default this course used and where to change it. One pass through Settings in each app: Field, Manager, then Office.

The map

One Settings screen per app

All three apps keep Settings as one screen of cards: open a card, change what you need, then Save All Changes at the bottom (Office saves as you go). A few cards repeat across the suite: Appearance, Company, Data & Backup, Help & User Guide. The rest match each app's job. Field carries the crew cards (Employee, Work & Pay, Jurisdiction & OHS Authority, Stat Holidays). Manager carries the PM cards (My Profile, Report Routing, Reminders). Office carries the desk cards (PDF & Quotes, Dashboard & Costing, Accounting, Estimate Templates, Security).

Field · Work & pay

The rules behind every timesheet

Dana's Part 8 timesheet did its math from this card. Pay schedule and the first period start date decide which pay period a shift lands in. Typical start and end times prefill new entries. rounds them: 1 hour keeps entry fast, 15 minutes if payroll wants it exact. The sets when hours turn into OT (Daily past a per-day threshold, Weekly past the week's cap, Both, or Custom), and the matches your province or state. From there the app flags OT on its own.

Field · Your data

Backups and the cloud folder, crew side

Three ways to back up: the cloud folder, email, or a file saved on the phone. Restore from Backup brings any of them back. is the manual door for dispatched jobs, and tapping a .morjob attachment in email does the same thing. With the Company Cloud Folder connected, imports dispatched jobs on its own; Check Cloud pulls on demand.

Manager · Report routing

Reports reach the right inbox by default

When a PM forwards crew reports from Manager, these addresses are already filled in: for timesheets and expenses, for FLHAs and incident reports, HR / Administration for general crew records. Default mail app picks which email app the phone opens.

Manager · Reminders

How hard a task nags

Task reminders behave by priority. Push is a normal notification. Alarm is the loud one: on Android it plays at alarm volume, on iPhone it breaks through Focus but not the mute switch. Badge is a count of due tasks on the Notes tab and the app icon. The sets the default mix; a task with a reminder always gets at least a push.

Backups, handoff and bulk import

Backups work like Field's. Two extras are PM-only. sends your full data set to MorForm Office, or to another project manager taking over (your own email is selected by default; add other PMs from your PM list). downloads a blank template to fill out in a spreadsheet and bulk-import into Jobs.
