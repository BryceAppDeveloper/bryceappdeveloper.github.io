A plain-English checklist of what a Certificate of Recognition (COR) audit looks for — the eight program elements, the documents auditors ask for, and the single most common reason crews lose points. Print it and walk your program before the auditor does.

What is COR — and why it pays off

A Certificate of Recognition (COR) verifies that a company's health & safety management system meets a provincial standard, audited annually. Beyond safety itself, COR is often required to bid public and larger private work — and in many provinces it can qualify you for WCB premium rebates.

10–20%Typical WCB rebate range where a partnership program offers one (varies by province & performance).
~12 monthsOf safety records auditors typically want to see in place before an audit.
#1 gapMissing or inconsistent daily FLHAs — the most common place crews lose points.

COR requirements, audit tools, and rebate programs are set by each province's certifying partner and WCB/WSIB. Figures above are general ranges, not a guarantee — confirm specifics with your provincial program. This page is guidance, not legal or financial advice.

The fastest win before any audit:

Make sure every crew has a signed FLHA for every shift, with no gaps in the dates. Auditors pull a date at random — a clean, continuous FLHA record is the easiest way to protect your score. Start with the free FLHA form →

The checklist — 8 elements

Most provincial audits map to these eight elements. Print this page (the checklist prints on its own) and tick what you can already produce.

COR Audit Prep Checklistmorform.app

1. Management leadership & commitment

  • Written, signed, dated health & safety policy (reviewed yearly)
  • Roles & responsibilities defined for management, supervisors, workers
  • Measurable annual safety objectives

2. Hazard identification & assessment

  • Formal hazard assessments for tasks/jobs (JHAs)
  • Daily field-level hazard assessments (FLHAs) — every crew, every shift
  • Hazard controls follow the hierarchy of controls

3. Hazard control

  • Safe work practices & procedures documented
  • PPE program and records
  • Preventive maintenance on tools/equipment

4. Inspections

  • Scheduled site/equipment inspections with records
  • Deficiencies tracked to closure with dates

5. Training, orientation & communication

  • New-worker & site orientations on file
  • Toolbox talks / safety meetings with sign-in sheets
  • Tickets & certifications tracked (WHMIS, first aid, etc.)

6. Incident investigation & reporting

  • Incident/near-miss reporting process
  • Investigations with root cause & corrective actions

7. Emergency response

  • Site-specific emergency response plan
  • Muster points, first aid, emergency contacts posted
  • Drills documented

8. Program administration & records

  • ~12 months of records organized & retrievable
  • Statistics tracked (incidents, inspections, talks)
  • Annual management review of the program
