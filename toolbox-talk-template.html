What is a toolbox talk?

A toolbox talk is a short, informal safety meeting, usually 5 to 10 minutes at the start of a shift, focused on one hazard relevant to the day's work. It keeps safety top-of-mind, surfaces near-misses, and creates a documented record of due diligence. You'll also hear it called a tailgate meeting, safety talk, or pre-job briefing.

Toolbox talk vs. FLHA

A toolbox talk is a crew discussion about one topic. An FLHA (Field-Level Hazard Assessment) is each crew's assessment of the hazards at their work front that day. Run the talk, then have the crew fill their FLHA. Grab the free FLHA form →

How to run one that actually works

  • Pick one topic tied to today's work. Relevance beats a generic lecture.
  • Keep it to 5-10 minutes. What the hazard is, why it matters here, what you'll do about it.
  • Make it two-way. Ask the crew what they've seen. They know the field risks.
  • Record attendance & actions. Everyone signs in; note hazards raised and who's following up.
  • Keep the record. A signed talk is evidence for COR audits and incident investigations.
