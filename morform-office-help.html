Welcome to MorForm Office

MorForm Office — Windows Desktop MorForm Office is the estimating and back-office hub of the MorForm suite. Run it on your office PC to build estimates and win work, generate client quotes, manage jobs, bill progress draws, review supervisor reports, export to QuickBooks, and track company performance — all without accounts, cloud storage, or subscriptions.

Everything is stored locally on your computer. Communication happens entirely through email attachments — three custom file types handle the whole workflow. .morjob dispatches jobs to supervisors. .morrep brings supervisor reports back to you. .mordata moves your full project data between Manager mobile and MorForm Office. No internet connection required to use the app.

What MorForm Office does

FeatureDescription
JobsCreate and manage job sites, assign PMs, add contacts and permits, dispatch to supervisors
EstimatesStandalone estimate pipeline — Draft → Approved → Quoted → Won / Lost — with labour, materials, sub costs, markup, scope of work, named templates, and custom columns. Winning an estimate creates the job.
Draws & QuickBooksBill progress draws against contract value, then export invoices, expenses, and time to QuickBooks (Online CSV or Desktop IIF)
CrewDirectory of your PMs, supervisors, safety officers, and employees
Contacts & VendorsSubcontractor and supplier directory — linked to estimate rows
Send & ReceiveEverything in and out — import and approve supervisor reports (.morrep), receive job requests from Manager mobile, dispatch jobs, and export to QuickBooks
Settings → Data & BackupImport .mordata from Manager mobile — merge or replace jobs, specs, PMs, crew, and notes
OverviewCompany dashboard — revenue, income, and won/lost by day, week, month, or year — plus budget vs. actuals per job

Jobs

Creating a Job

Click + New Job in the top right. Fill in:

Job name Address Start & end dates Status Scope notes Project Manager Supervisor Site contacts Permits Photos Documents (PDF)
💡 Click anywhere on a job row to open it for editing — no need to hunt for the Edit button. Closing an editor with unsaved changes asks whether to save first.

Job Status

🟢 Active
→
🔵 Complete
or

Change status any time by editing the job. Status affects how it appears in Overview — completing a job also books its remaining contract value as revenue.

Deleting & Restoring Jobs

Deleting a job moves it to the Deleted tab at the top of the Jobs page rather than removing it outright. It stays there for 30 days with the days remaining shown — Restore it to Active, or Delete permanently. After 30 days it's removed automatically.

Bulk Add Jobs

Use 📥 Bulk Add Jobs from Spreadsheet to create multiple jobs at once from a CSV or XLSX — one row per job, handy when first moving an existing job list into MorForm. Download the template from Settings → Data & Backup first. Jobs started in MorForm Manager mobile arrive via Send & Receive instead.

Site Contacts & Permits

Add site contacts (owner, inspector, safety officer) directly to the job. Add permits with permit type and expiry date. Both are included in the dispatch package sent to the supervisor.

💡 Photos and PDF documents attached to a job (site plans, drawings) are bundled into the .morjob dispatch. The supervisor sees them on their phone.

Dispatching Jobs

When a job is ready to go to site, dispatch it to a supervisor. They receive a .morjob file by email and import it into the MorForm app on their phone in one tap.

1
Open the job and click 📤 Dispatch
Found in the job detail view.
2
Select a supervisor
Choose from your Crew directory. Their email pre-fills in the compose window.
3
Choose what to include
Contacts, photos, and documents are selectable. Uncheck anything you don't want to send.
4
Send Job Package
MorForm saves the .morjob file and highlights it in your file explorer, then opens your default mail client with the supervisor's email pre-filled. Desktop email can't attach files on its own, so attach the highlighted .morjob file to the email and send. (Tip: connect a Company Cloud Folder and dispatched jobs deliver automatically — no attaching needed.)

What's in the dispatch package

Job name & address Start & end dates Scope notes PM contact info Site contacts Trade scopes from estimate Permits Site photos Plans & documents
⚠️ If the package exceeds ~15 MB you'll be warned. Large PDF drawings are the usual cause — consider compressing them first.

Estimates

Estimates are standalone — build one for any bid, whether or not a job exists yet. Create one with + New Estimate on the Estimates screen, or convert a job request sent from Manager mobile (see Send & Receive). When you create one, pick a starting point from the Start from template dropdown — no Excel import needed.

Estimate pipeline

Draft
→
Approved
→
Quoted
→
🏆 Won / ✖ Lost

Mark an estimate Approved when the numbers are ready. Exporting a Proposal & Quote (or clicking 📤 Mark Quoted) moves it to Quoted. When the client says yes, click 🏆 Won — Create Job — the job is created from the estimate automatically, with every trade's scope checklist ready to dispatch. Mark it ✖ Lost and it's kept for your win/loss stats on the Overview dashboard.

Templates

The Start from template dropdown is ordered Blank (MorForm starter) → a built-in gallery (General Contractor — Residential & Commercial, plus Plumbing, Electrical, Structural, Interior Systems, and HVAC) → your own saved templates. The Blank sheet shows greyed guideline text so you can see what goes where — type over it or leave fields blank. Set the default for new estimates and 📤 export any template to a fill-in Excel sheet in Settings → Estimate Templates.

Save any estimate as a named template and tag it by job type (e.g. Dental clinic, Whitebox). 💾 Save layout as my template keeps your divisions, line items, and custom columns for every new estimate.

Smart suggestions

As you fill out estimates and contacts, MorForm learns the division names, line items, and trades you use. Division headers, the Description column, and a contact's Role/Trade field all offer a type-or-pick dropdown — start typing to reuse an existing name or just type a new one. Line-item suggestions are tuned to the division you're in (e.g. plumbing items under a Plumbing division).

✏ Edit Layout — custom columns

Click ✏ Edit Layout in the top bar to change the sheet itself: rename any column, hide ones you don't use, reorder them, or + Add column. New columns can be a dollar amount (adds to the row's Sub Total), a % of the row (like Markup), a % of the whole estimate (added at the bottom — e.g. a fee), or plain words. Custom columns survive XLSX export and re-import.

Scope of work (📝)

Click the 📝 icon on any row to add a scope description for that trade. Scope text is included in the .morjob dispatch — the supervisor sees exactly what each trade is on site to do. When a supervisor marks a scope complete it comes back in their .morrep report and updates the estimate automatically.

📝 The 📝 icon turns gold when a scope is written, and green when the supervisor marks it complete. Rows with complete scope have a subtle green background.

Division headers (collapsible)

Click + Division to add a collapsible division divider (e.g. Electrical, Finishes). Each division header has a ▼ / ▶ caret to collapse or expand its line items — handy on long estimates — and a ➕ to add a line item inside that division. Divisions organise the sheet but don't affect totals.

Default markup

Set a project-wide default markup percentage at the top of the estimate. Individual rows can override it — leave the Markup % field blank on a row to use the default.

XLSX import & export

Export any estimate to Excel for sharing or printing. Import an XLSX to load rows from an external spreadsheet — every import shows a preview first (divisions and rows) so you can check it before anything is saved.

📥 Best workflow: File → Export Blank Estimate Template, fill it in, and import that file. MorForm-exported files carry a hidden signature so they re-import exactly — no guessing. Other spreadsheets are matched by column names (Division, Description, Contractor, Workers, Hours, Days, Rate, Mat'l, Sub, Markup); if the file doesn't match, the preview warns you before you commit.

How to fill in the blank template

In the exported template, fill the columns marked type here and leave the rest blank — MorForm calculates Labor and every total for you.

ColumnWhat to do
DivisionType here — a division name on its own row (e.g. In-House). Leave the rest of that row blank.
DescriptionType here — the line-item name (e.g. Supervision). Leave Division blank on item rows.
Contractor/VendorType here (optional) — who does the work.
Workers / Hours / Days / RateType here — labour inputs; MorForm multiplies them into Labor.
LaborLeave blank — calculated automatically.
Mat'l / SubType here (optional) — material and subcontractor dollar amounts.
ScopeType here (optional) — notes about the work.

Don't have a number yet? Leave it blank — empty cells just add $0.

💡 If you type a vendor name in the estimate that doesn't exist in Contacts yet, it's automatically added as a stub with a ⚠ Add info badge. Fill in their details in the Contacts screen.

Proposal & Quote (PDF / XLSX)

MorForm Office exports client-ready Proposal & Quote documents as real PDF or XLSX files, with your logo, terms, and signature.

1
Set up PDF settings
In Settings → PDF & Quotes — configure your quote number format, validity period, standard terms, and upload a signature photo.
2
Approve the estimate, then click 📄 Proposal & Quote
Pick PDF or XLSX, set the client name and cover notes, and choose how much price detail the client sees — division subtotals, every line item, or grand total only. Internal costs and markup never appear.
3
Save the file
The PDF is generated directly — just pick where to save it. Exporting marks the estimate Quoted and increments the quote counter.

Quote numbering

🔢 Auto mode formats quote numbers as Q-YYYY-NNN and increments the counter each time you export. Manual mode lets you type a number each time. Use the Override field to set a specific next number — it clears automatically after one use.

Signature upload

Take a photo of your handwritten signature on white paper. Settings → PDF & Quotes → Upload Signature opens an editor where you can rotate and crop the photo, then the background is automatically removed to give you a clean transparent signature for quotes.

Managers & report routing

Add a Manager for each person who should receive field reports — typically a primary manager for timesheets, an account manager for expenses, and a safety manager for safety forms. Then open Settings → 📧 Report Routing and choose which manager receives each kind. When a supervisor's report comes in, the report viewer shows a one-click 📧 Forward button for timesheets, expenses, and safety forms that opens your email pre-filled to the right manager with a summary.

💡 Supervisors are created automatically the first time you import one of their reports. You don't need to add them manually before they send their first .morrep.

Send & Receive

Everything that moves in or out of MorForm Office lives on this tab (formerly called Inbox) — supervisor reports in, job dispatches out, Manager job requests in, QuickBooks exports out. Supervisors submit pay period reports from the MorForm app on their phone; those arrive in your email as .morrep file attachments.

🧾The QuickBooks Export row at the bottom of the Import & Export panel offers the same Invoices / Expenses / Time CSVs and Desktop IIF as Settings → Accounting — they're also in the File menu.

Import from Manager Mobile

MorForm Office can receive a full data snapshot from MorForm Manager mobile. This lets you continue work on a larger screen, or receive a job handoff from another project manager.

1
On Manager mobile — Settings → Data & Backup → Send to Office / Handoff
Sends a .mordata file to your email (and optionally other PMs).
2
Save the .mordata attachment to your computer
3
In MorForm Office — Settings → Data & Backup → 📱 Import .mordata
4
Choose Merge or Replace All
Merge adds new jobs, PMs, crew and notes without touching existing data. Replace All wipes current data and loads the snapshot — double confirmed before proceeding.
💡The .mordata file contains all jobs, specs / job requests, budgets, PMs, supervisors, and notes. Supervisor submission details are included as summaries only.

Import a dispatched job (.morjob)

A .morjob is a single job dispatched from MorForm Manager mobile or another copy of Office — it carries the job's details, contacts, trade scopes, permits, photos, and documents. Office usually sends these to supervisors, but you can also pull one in (for example, a job a colleague dispatched to you, or one recovered from an old email).

1
Save the .morjob attachment to your computer
2
In MorForm Office — Data → 📦 Import Job (.morjob)
Or use the 📂 Import & Export panel on the Send & Receive tab. Pick the file.
3
The job lands on your Jobs tab
With its contacts, trade scopes, permits, photos, and documents attached, and the project manager linked. If a job with the same ID already exists you're asked to Replace it; a job sitting in Deleted is offered back as Restore & Update.
💡A .morjob carries the dispatched job only — not cost or hours data, which live in the estimate. Imported trade scopes ride along on the job's contacts so they survive a re-dispatch.

☁️ Company Cloud Folder (optional)

Don't want to email files back and forth? Connect a shared folder in your company Drive / Dropbox / OneDrive and the apps pass the same .morrep / .mordata files through it instead. There's no MorForm account and no server to host — it rides on your own cloud provider's sync app plus the file system, so your data stays in your accounts.

First, choose how accounts work — tap a card to see its steps:

A · One company account — one login on every device, no sharing step. Everyone can see all the files (best for small, trusted crews).

  1. Make one cloud account for the company (e.g. a Google account just for MorForm).
  2. Install that provider's app on the office PC and every phone, signed in with that account.
  3. Create a folder called MorForm in it.
  4. In Office: Data → Connect Company Cloud Folder → pick it. On each phone: MorForm → Connect Company Cloud Folder → pick the same folder.

B · Everyone uses their own account — you share each person only their own folder. Nobody sees anyone else's files. A little more setup; easy to remove someone later.

  1. On the office PC, create a folder called MorForm in your cloud.
  2. Right-click it → Share → add each crew member's email with edit access. For strict privacy, share each person their own subfolder instead of the whole folder.
  3. Each person opens the invite and chooses Add to my Drive / Add to my files so it syncs to their device.
  4. In Office: Data → Connect Company Cloud Folder → pick it. On each phone: MorForm → Connect Company Cloud Folder → pick the shared folder.

Then, day to day:

1
Set it up once in Office
Have a Drive/Dropbox/OneDrive folder synced on the office PC, then Settings → Data & Backup → Connect Company Cloud Folder. Office auto-creates an inbox for each crew member from your contacts.
2
The crew connect once
On each phone, the same folder is synced (company cloud account or a shared folder), then in MorForm they tap Connect Company Cloud Folder and pick it. That's the only step they do.
3
Files flow on their own
Reports/data dropped into the shared folder import automatically (same Merge / Replace prompts), routed to the right person's inbox and stamped with who sent them. Desktop is automatic; Android scans when opened; iPhone imports via the Files app.
💡Email keeps working exactly as before — the cloud folder is an extra channel, not a replacement. A shared folder means everyone with access can see the files; for strict per-person privacy you can lock individual subfolders in your cloud's sharing settings.
💾Once a cloud folder is connected, Settings → Data & Backup → Back Up to Cloud Folder drops a timestamped .morbak backup into a backups subfolder in one tap — no save dialog. It syncs through your cloud app like everything else, and stays out of the crew inboxes so no app tries to import it.

📐 Job requests from Manager mobile

When a PM specs a job in MorForm Manager (job → type → subtype → trades & scope) and taps Send to Office, the spec arrives in the .mordata import and lands here as a job request. One click converts it into a full estimate — every trade becomes a division with its scope checklist attached. The job itself is created when the estimate is Won — under the same job id the PM's phone uses, so sending a .mordata back (Data → Send Data to Manager) marks the job ✅ Approved in their app and the supervisor's .morrep reports match the job in both places.

Importing a report

1
Save the attachment
In your email client, save the .morrep file to your computer (e.g. Downloads folder).
2
Click 📥 Import .morrep
Browse to the saved file and open it. The report appears in the Inbox list.
3
Review
Click the report to open it. Review hours (regular and overtime), expenses, safety documents, trade scope progress, and the site photo grid.

What gets committed on approval

Forwarding to managers

An open report has a 📧 Forward to managers section with one-click buttons for timesheets, expenses, and safety forms. Each opens your default mail client pre-filled — with a summary of that part — to the manager you assigned in Settings → Report Routing. Add those managers first on the Crew screen.

Incident Report Sign-off

🚨 When a report contains an incident report, a red 🚨 INCIDENT badge appears on the report row. Alberta OH&S requires the employer to investigate reportable incidents and document corrective actions. The sign-off in MorForm creates this record.

Open the report — a dedicated red sign-off section appears below the safety documents. For each incident:

1
Review the incident details
The supervisor's incident title, date, and description are shown.
2
Enter corrective actions
Describe what was done, root cause, who is responsible for follow-up, and by when. Be specific — this is your investigation record.
3
Click ✅ Sign Off
Records your name, timestamp, and corrective action notes permanently.
4
Click 📄 Export PDF
Generates a full incident report document including your sign-off. Opens in your browser — use File → Print → Save as PDF. Keep this on file for OHS compliance. Can be provided to an OHS officer on request.
ℹ️ Incident sign-off is separate from report approval. Sign off the incident first, then approve the full report. Sign-off cannot be undone.

Updated reports (re-importing)

Supervisors can re-send the same job's report as work progresses — importing the newer file prompts Replace? and swaps in the updated report (scope progress and photos update, and it returns to pending for re-review). If the earlier version was already approved, its committed hours and expenses are backed out automatically — re-review and approve the new one. No double counting.

Overview

Overview is the company dashboard. Pick a granularity — day, week, month, or year — and see Revenue, Costs, Income, Labour hours, and estimates Won / Lost for each period, with stat cards for the totals (including your win rate). Below it, every job shows budget vs. actuals — click a job row to open its full dashboard.

How the numbers are built

NumberSource
RevenueProgress draws as you log them, plus the remaining contract value when a job completes — never double-counted
CostsApproved expenses plus labour hours at your labour cost rate (set in Settings)
IncomeRevenue minus costs
Won / LostEstimate decisions dated in the period — your win rate over time

💵 Draws & contract value

On the job dashboard, set the job's contract value (a Won estimate's total is used automatically) and log draws — progress billing — as you invoice the client (e.g. "Draw 2 — rough-in complete"). Draws count as revenue on the Overview dashboard and feed the QuickBooks invoice export.

🧾 QuickBooks export

Settings → Accounting exports files you import into QuickBooks yourself — no cloud connection. QBO Invoices CSV (one invoice line per draw), QBO Expenses CSV (approved supervisor expenses), and QBO Time Activities CSV (approved hours) import into QuickBooks Online. The QuickBooks Desktop IIF file imports via File → Utilities → Import → IIF Files — test in a sample company first, IIF imports can't be undone. Account names are configurable and must match your QuickBooks chart of accounts. Exported draws are stamped, so you're warned before exporting the same draw twice.

File Formats

FormatDirectionDescription
.morjob Office / Manager → Supervisor Job dispatch package. Contains job details, contacts, permits, trade scopes, photos, and documents. Office mainly sends these to supervisors, but can also import one in via Data → Import Job (.morjob). JSON format.
.morrep Supervisor → Office / Manager Pay period report. Contains time entries, expenses, safety forms, and scope completion statuses. JSON format.
.mordata Manager ↔ Office Data snapshot between Manager mobile and Office — jobs, specs / job requests, budgets, crew, notes. JSON format.
.morbak Backup (this app) Full-app backup of everything in MorForm Office, for safekeeping or moving to a new computer. Restore via Settings → Data & Backup or the first-run setup screen. App-specific — a mobile backup can't restore into Office. JSON format.
.xlsx Import / Export Estimate import and export. Also used for bulk job import when formatted as the job template.
.csv Import / Export Bulk job import using the MorForm job template, and QuickBooks Online export files (invoices, expenses, time).
.json Backup Full app data backup. All jobs, estimates, crew, contacts, reports, and settings in one file.
💡 Both .morjob and .morrep are plain JSON files. If you ever need to inspect or troubleshoot one, you can open it in any text editor.
