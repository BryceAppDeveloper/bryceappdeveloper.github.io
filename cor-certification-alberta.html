How to Get COR Certified in Alberta

If you run a construction company in Alberta, you've probably hit a wall that has nothing to do with the quality of your work: a general contractor or a public owner won't let you bid because you don't have COR. Here's a plain-English guide to what it is, how to get it, and the one thing that trips up most crews.

The steps ↓ Get the COR audit checklist →

What is COR — and why it's worth the effort

COR stands for Certificate of Recognition. It's a nationally recognized standard that says your company has a health and safety management system that meets provincial requirements and has been verified by an external audit. In Alberta it's issued through Certifying Partners (industry safety associations such as the Alberta Construction Safety Association) under the province's Partnerships in Injury Reduction (PIR) program.

Two reasons it's worth doing:

  • It's the ticket to bid. COR (or SECOR, the version for small employers) is the practical prequalification for most public, institutional, and large private projects. No COR, no bid list.
  • It can lower your costs. Where the province offers it, maintaining a COR can earn a workers'-comp premium rebate — commonly in the 10–20% range.

Rebate programs and amounts vary by province and change over time — confirm the current Alberta PIR details with WCB. This page is guidance, not legal or financial advice.

The steps to get COR

Getting certified is less about being safer — most crews already work safely — and more about building and proving a system. Roughly:

1. BuildA health & safety management system: written policies, hazard assessments, safe work practices, inspections, incident reporting, training records, emergency response.
2. Run itImplement it on real jobs for about a year — auditors want roughly 12 months of records: actual completed forms, not a binder of blank templates.
3. Pass the auditA certified auditor reviews documents, interviews your people, and observes the worksite. You generally need 80% overall and at least 50% on every element.
4. MaintainAnnual maintenance audits keep the certificate current, with a full re-audit every few years.

What the audit actually checks

A COR audit pulls evidence from three places:

  • Documentation review — policies, training records, inspection logs, incident reports, meeting minutes, and your daily FLHA (Field Level Hazard Assessment) forms.
  • Worker interviews — from frontline crew to management, about how hazards are managed day to day.
  • Worksite observation — checking that what's written down matches what's actually happening on the ground.

The #1 reason companies lose points

Ask any auditor and you'll hear the same thing: the most common gap isn't unsafe work — it's the daily FLHA. The formal, pre-planned hazard assessments are usually on file in the trailer, but the field-level assessments are missing, incomplete, or not done before work starts. That's where the points slip away.

A COR audit is, more than anything, a filing problem: can you show, across the last 12 months, that hazards were assessed and controlled before each task — with the form, the control, the sign-off, and the date? Start with the free FLHA form →

Make the paperwork build itself

The crews that breeze through audits aren't doing more safety work — they're capturing it as they go instead of reconstructing it at audit time. A few habits that pay off:

  • Fill the FLHA before work starts, on the phone, not from memory at the end of the day.
  • Timestamp it and record the control action taken, not just the hazard.
  • Store it somewhere the office can pull it — not a damp clipboard in the truck.

Common questions

How long does it take to get COR?
Plan on the better part of a year — you need ~12 months of records before the certification audit.

Is COR legally required?
No, but it's effectively required to bid most large and public projects, so in practice many contractors can't grow without it.

What's SECOR?
The small-employer version of COR, designed for companies with a handful of workers.

Does COR actually save money?
Beyond winning more work, COR can qualify you for WCB premium rebates where the province offers them — confirm the current Alberta PIR figures with WCB.
