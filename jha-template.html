What is a JHA?

A Job Hazard Analysis (also called a Job Safety Analysis, or JSA) breaks a task into its basic steps, identifies the hazard in each step, and assigns a control before the work starts. It's one of the simplest, most effective tools for preventing incidents on a construction site, and it's a document safety auditors expect to see.

JHA vs. FLHA: what's the difference?

A JHA is task-based: it's prepared for a specific job or activity and often reused. An FLHA (Field-Level Hazard Assessment) is done by the crew at the work front, the day of, for the conditions in front of them. Most sites use both: JHAs for planning, FLHAs daily. Grab the free FLHA form here.

How to complete a JHA (5 steps)

  1. Describe the job

    Write the task, location, date, crew, and supervisor at the top. Be specific: "set forms, north footing" beats "concrete work."

  2. Break the job into steps

    List each basic step in the order it happens. Aim for the natural chunks of the task, not every hand movement.

  3. Identify the hazards

    For each step, ask what could cause harm: falls, pinch points, electrical, struck-by, dust/silica, traffic, heat, weather, manual lifting.

  4. Assign a control

    Choose the strongest practical control using the hierarchy of controls: eliminate → substitute → engineering → administrative → PPE. PPE is the last line, not the first.

  5. Assign responsibility & sign

    Name who's responsible for each control, then have every worker review and sign before starting. The signatures are what turn a form into a real safety conversation.

Common construction hazards & controls

Working at heights

Guardrails or a tied-off harness; inspect anchors; cover or barricade openings.

Struck-by / mobile equipment

Spotters, exclusion zones, high-vis, eye contact before moving.

Silica & dust

Wet-cutting or on-tool extraction; respirators where needed.

Electrical

Lock-out/tag-out, GFCIs, locate before digging, assume live.

Manual handling

Team lifts, mechanical aids, break loads down.

Weather / heat & cold

Water and shade, warm-up breaks, reschedule high-wind work.
